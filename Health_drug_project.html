What are E-cigarettes?

E-cigarettes usually contain a battery, a heating device, and a liquid storage compartment. They can be used to deliver marijuana and other drugs.

E-cigarettes are not safe for youth, young adults, and pregnant women, as well as adults who do not currently use tobacco products.

Short term consequences and the dangers of using:

  • Acute nicotine exposure can be toxic, and children and adults alike have been poisoned from exposure through the eyes and skin to the liquid found in e-cigarettes.
  • Other substances found in e-cigarettes include cancer causing chemicals and tiny particles in the aerosol which reach deep into the lungs during inhalation.

Long term consequences:

  • The long term effects of using e-cigarettes are mostly unknown, as they have not been around long enough to be thoroughly studied. However, it is suspected that these long term effects may be similar to the long term effects of cigarette use, which includes cancer, lung disease, heart disease, and diabetes, among others.

What does it look like?

Some e-cigarettes are designed to look like regular cigarettes or cigars, while others may look like pens, USB-sticks, or other everyday items.
