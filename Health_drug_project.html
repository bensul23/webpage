What are E-cigarettes?

E-cigarettes usually contain a battery, a heating device, and a liquid storage compartment. They can be used to deliver marijuana and other drugs.

How do they affect your body and brain?

  • Nicotine can hinder brain development in teenagers and young adults even into their early 20’s.
  • The nicotine in e-cigarettes is highly addictive.
  • Nicotine is toxic to developing fetuses.

E-cigarettes are not safe for youth, young adults, and pregnant women, as well as adults who do not currently use tobacco products.

Short term consequences and the dangers of using:

  • Acute nicotine exposure can be toxic, and children and adults alike have been poisoned from exposure through the eyes and skin to the liquid found in e-cigarettes.
  • Other substances found in e-cigarettes include cancer causing chemicals and tiny particles in the aerosol which reach deep into the lungs during inhalation.

Long term consequences:

  • The long term effects of using e-cigarettes are mostly unknown, as they have not been around long enough to be thoroughly studied. However, it is suspected that these long term effects may be similar to the long term effects of cigarette use, which includes cancer, lung disease, heart disease, and diabetes, among others.

What does it look like?

Some e-cigarettes are designed to look like regular cigarettes or cigars, while others may look like pens, USB-sticks, or other everyday items.

How is it administered?

  • They work by heating up a liquid composed of flavoring, nicotine (or other addictive drugs), and chemicals to make an aerosol which is inhaled into the lungs.
  • It is possible to get secondhand smoke from this gas.

What is it derived from?

E-cigarettes may contain:

  • Nicotine
  • Ultrafine particles that can be inhaled deep into the lungs
  • Flavoring such as diacetyl, a chemical linked to a serious lung disease
  • Volatile organic compounds
  • Cancer-causing chemicals
  • Heavy metals such as nickel, tin, and lead

Classification:

Non-nicotine containing e-cigarettes are considered consumer products. Nicotine-containing e-cigarettes are classified as tobacco products, thus their sale is prohibited to minors.

Street names for E-cigarettes

E Cigarettes may be known by many different names, such as:

  • E-cigs
  • E-hookahs
  • mods
  • tank systems
  • vape pens
  • vapes
  • ENDS (electronic nicotine delivery systems)

Treatment:

  • Drugs are difficult to quit due to the addictive substances they contain such as nicotine, which changes your brain chemistry to seek out more of the substance.
  • E-cigarettes are often used as a substitute for more extreme substances such as cigarettes, but they are not currently approved by the FDA as a quit smoking aid.
  • Treatment for E-cigarette abuse involves a counselor doing an assessment of the abuser’s family and then forming a treatment plan to gradually reduce their use of the substance until they no longer need it.
